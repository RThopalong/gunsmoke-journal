April 26, 2004. Echo Company 2/1

April 26, 2016

Probably the single hardest fought battle of the 2004 deployment was on the morning of April 26, 2004 between elements of Echo Company and a large insurgent force. I have seen estimates of insurgent strengths from 'only' 75 up to 200 - both numbers easily outnumbered 2nd platoon, even with attachments. Echo company was led by Captain Doug Zembiec and 1st Sgt Bill Skiles - both men were proven combat leaders with aggressive natures, and second platoon was staffed by a once in a lifetime mix of excellent squad leaders, corpsmen and a top notch machine gun team.

Embedded with 2nd platoon was a war correspondent from the LA Times, Rick Loomis. Rick was primarily a photographer, but for this very emotional battle stepped out of his comfort zone to write an excellent article of the fighting titled "Imagine Dying," published in the LA Times Magazine.

2nd Platoon and attachments took over a dozen injuries, 4 life threatening, and Aaron Austin, a popular and hard charging machine gunner, was killed.

Echo Company's CO, Captain Zembiec, later wrote home describing the intensity of that day's fighting - dozens of RPGs and thousands of rounds of enemy machine gun fire in the opening minutes, insurgents closing to within meters of the Marines' position before being forced back, and the desperate effort to save LCpl Austin after he was hit. Captain Zembiec credited the valor of his Marines and Corpsmen for the outcome of the fight, and mourned Austin as a warrior who died throwing a grenade at the enemy.

Here is a video link to some audio taken during the fight by Rick Loomis, and some interviews done after the fighting.

Corpsman Doc Duty later shared a detailed account of the casualty evacuation that day, describing how the wounded, including Aaron Austin, were rushed to the Casualty Collection Point and then to the Battalion Aid Station. Despite extensive efforts, including CPR performed the entire drive to the hospital, Austin succumbed to his wounds. His account was shared with Aaron's mother, De'on Miller, with her permission.

LCpl Carlos Gomez-Perez, LCpl Thomas Adametz, and LCpl Aaron Austin (posthumously) were awarded the Silver Star for gallantry in action. They fought wounded, outnumbered, and flanked by a determined enemy. They, and the rest of the men from 2nd platoon, fought like lions. Semper Fidelis.

Ryan.
